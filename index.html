What we do

Whether it's a cherished photograph, a hard-earned diploma, an original painting, or a stretched canvas, each frame is cut from carefully selected hardwood, joined, and finished by hand to your exact specifications.

Photo Framing

From standard prints to large-format photographs, we make elegant hardwood frames precisely cut with the profile of your choice. Complement your image without competing with it with an archival mat of the color of your choice.

Our craft

We source domestic hardwoods wherever possible and readily propose alternatives to vulnerable species (e.g., utile/sapele instead of mahogany). The wood is cut to order on a compound miter saw, precisely carved to various profiles using a router table, and joined with dowels and wood glue for tight, gap-free corners.

All frames are then inspected for square and quality and then sanded to 180-240 grit, depending on the wood species and finish. Glass and backing are hand-cut to fit the frame and glass is inspected for scratches and cloudiness.

We comply with conservation mounting standards: no acidic tapes and acid-free and lignin-free mats. Our mat boards are 4-ply and feature a tasteful 45-degree beveled cutout. D-rings and plastic-coated hanging wire are rated for the frame's weight.
